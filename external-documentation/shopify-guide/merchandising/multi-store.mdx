title: "Multi-store"
description: "Merchandise once on your main store and replicate it across your connected Shopify stores"
---

Multi-store lets you run several Shopify stores and manage their Depict merchandising from a single place. You do the work once on your **main store** — set your sorting, pin products, add content cards — and Depict mirrors those actions onto every **connected store**. No redoing the same job on each store.

<Note>Multi-store is available on the **Pro** and **Custom** plans.</Note>

## The short version

You nominate one store as your main, then connect your other Shopify stores to it using an activation code. Once connected, the stores form a group. Collections that exist on more than one store get **linked**, and the merchandising you do on the source store of a linked collection is replicated to the others automatically.

## What it does

- **Replicates your merchandising.** Sort order, pinned products, and content cards you set on the source store of a linked collection are copied to the other stores in the group.
- **Links collections across stores by handle.** A collection counts as "the same" collection across stores when it shares the same collection handle. Those matching collections are grouped together so they can be merchandised as one.
- **Matches products across stores by SKU.** When merchandising is replicated, Depict figures out which product on Store B corresponds to the one you pinned on Store A by matching SKUs — so a pin or a product card lands on the right product even though each store has its own product IDs.
- **Keeps each store's catalog separate.** Every store still connects to its own Shopify and ingests its own products. Multi-store shares the *merchandising decisions*, not the catalogs.

## How to set it up

You'll generate a code on your main store and enter it while installing Depict on the store you want to connect.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create and copy an activation code">
    In **Multi-store settings** on your main store, add a new activation code, then copy it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install Depict on the store you're connecting">
    Go to Shopify, switch to the store you want to connect, and install Depict.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the code">
    On the plan-selection screen, choose **"Enter store activation code"**, paste the code you saved, and press **"Activate store"**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The store is now connected. Repeat for any other store you want to add to the group.

After stores are connected, open **Multi-store → Collections** to see which collections are linked across the group and to choose, for each linked collection, **which store is the source** that the others replicate from.

## Benefits

- **Do the work once.** Merchandise your main store and let Depict handle the rest — a big time saver when you run the same brand across several storefronts.
- **Consistency across storefronts.** Linked collections stay in sync, so customers get the same curated experience whichever store they land on.
- **No manual re-pinning.** SKU matching means you don't have to find and re-pin the same product on every store by hand.

## Caveats & limitations

- **Pro or Custom plan only.** Multi-store isn't available on the lower tiers.
- **Collections link by handle.** Two collections are only treated as the same collection across stores when their handles match. If a collection has a different handle on one store, it won't link automatically.
- **Products map by SKU.** A product can only be matched across stores if it shares a SKU. Products with missing or mismatched SKUs won't carry their merchandising across.
- **You can't disconnect a store that's still a source.** If a store is set as the replication source for any linked collection, disconnecting it is blocked until you reassign those collections to a different source.
- **Not everything replicates.** Replication covers your standard collection merchandising. Special cases — for example, most metaobject-based setups, and any **market-specific** versions of a collection — are handled per store.
